When life gives you lemons, what do you do?

In this summer memoir, Dave Leland dabbles in lemony days and distinct landmarks. Bask in the sun-soaked, citrus-infused, and heartfelt recipes I wrote for the body and soul.

Let there be lemon bars!

My dad had an indoor lemon tree in his New York home, and an oven that hasn't been used in months. I was 19, and made these for the first time. He raved about it so much, it ended up being our holiday gift that year. I could smell lemon for weeks.

These luscious lemon bars are the perfect Christmas morning starters or a showstopper dessert for a weekend dinner. It's a little sweet, a little tart, all coming together for custard, lemon-y perfection.

You'll need:
  • Granulated sugar, 2 1/2 cups
  • All-purpose flour, 2 cups & 8 tbsp
  • Large eggs, 6 pcs
  • Lemon juice, 1 cup
  • Confectioner's sugar, optional 3 tbsp
  • Unsalted butter, 1 cup melted
  • Pure vanilla extract, 2 tsp
  • Salt, 1/2 tsp
Get started:
  1. Preheat the oven to 325 F or 163 C.
  2. Mix the melted butter, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt.
  3. Add the flour and stir completely.
  4. Press firmly into prepared pan, making sure it's even.
  5. Bake crust for 20-22 minutes until golden.
  6. Sift sugar and flour in a large bowl.
  7. Whisk in eggs then lemon juice until combined.
  8. Pour filling over warm crust.
  9. Bake the bars for 22-26 minutes, until it no longer jiggles.
  10. Very important: let it cool before lifting from pan. Dust confectioner's sugar if preferred.

Let loose with limoncello!

In a train from Florence to Rome, I was offered coffee or limoncello. I was just 18 at the time. Naturally, everyone around me had limoncello, and I was distraught. My mom let me take a sip, as she said she did not care for it. But I did. And I've tried recreating it since then.

This limoncello recipe is simple, but bold. Best enjoyed under the summer heat, with the people you love. It feels just like a hug.

You'll need:
  • Lemons, 10 pcs
  • Vodka, 1 bottle, 750 ml
  • Granulated sugar, 1-4 cups, to taste
Get started:
  1. Remove the peel (outer yellow skin only) of the lemon.
  2. Transfer the lemon peels to a jar with a lid.
  3. Cover the peels with vodka.
  4. Screw on the lid.
  5. Let vodka and lemon peels infuse away from light for at least 4 days.
  6. Keep infusing, trust me. The best ones are infused for a month.
  7. Strain the vodka.
  8. Prepare a syrup with equal parts water and sugar.
  9. Gradually add syrup to your limoncello, to taste.
